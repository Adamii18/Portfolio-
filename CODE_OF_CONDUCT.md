# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. The Jaidatech Ventures project and its community are dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being Respectful** - Use welcoming and inclusive language
- **Being Appreciative** - Show gratitude for others' contributions
- **Being Constructive** - Provide helpful feedback and suggestions
- **Being Collaborative** - Work together toward common goals
- **Being Patient** - Understand that people have different skill levels
- **Being Professional** - Focus on what is best for the community

### Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behaviors include:

- Harassment or intimidation of any kind
- Discriminatory jokes and language
- Posting sexually explicit or violent content
- Personal attacks or insults
- Unwanted sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, flaming, or baiting
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate
- Doxxing (sharing private information without consent)
- Brigading or coordinated attacks

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior
- Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently banning any contributor whose behavior is deemed inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples of representing a project include using an official project email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Reporting Guidelines

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

1. **Direct Contact** - Email the project maintainer directly
2. **GitHub Issues** - Create a private security advisory if applicable
3. **Document Details** - Include:
   - What happened
   - Who was involved
   - When it occurred
   - Any evidence (screenshots, links, etc.)
   - Your contact information

### Confidentiality

All reports will be kept confidential. We will investigate all complaints and respond appropriately.

## Handling Violations

When a violation is reported:

1. **Investigation** - We will investigate the complaint
2. **Communication** - We may reach out to the person reported for their side
3. **Resolution** - We will determine appropriate action
4. **Notification** - We will inform the reporter of the outcome

### Possible Responses

Depending on severity, responses may include:

- **Warning** - Private conversation about the issue
- **Temporary Ban** - Removal from project for a set period
- **Permanent Ban** - Removal from project indefinitely
- **Legal Action** - In severe cases involving harassment or threats

## Appeal Process

If you believe you have been unfairly treated:

1. Contact the project maintainer in writing
2. Provide your perspective and evidence
3. We will review and respond within 7 days
